A species is disappearing, and they're looking at disconnected data points instead of a unified story.
>
> **EcoScan changes that.**"

## The Problem Statement (10 seconds)
*[Show the problem visually by highlighting the three disconnected data sources]*

> "Traditional conservation tools do one thing well: they identify a plant or animal. But they can't answer the critical question: *Why is this species under pressure? What specific habitat stressors are causing the decline?*
>
> That gap has never been filled—until now."

---

## The Solution (20 seconds)
*[Run the guided demo with sample data]*

> "EcoScan fuses three streams of intelligence:
>
> **First:** Satellite data that shows us vegetation health, temperature stress, and moisture patterns across a habitat.
>
> **Second:** Distributed sensor networks—real air quality, humidity, soil chemistry—that ground-truth what's actually happening on the land.
>
> **Third:** Field photos *you* upload, which our AI analyzes to spot species and confirm what the data is telling us.

> Then—and this is the game-changing part—we tell you *exactly why* that species is in trouble. Not just 'monarch butterfly at risk.' We say: 'Monarch at risk because vegetation stress is 78%, thermal stress is high, and moisture is depleted—here's what you can do.'"

## Talking Points (If Challenged)

### "Why can't existing tools do this?"
- **Phyto Vision** and similar systems excel at species detection but ignore habitat context
- **Satellite tools** (NDVI, temperature mapping) lack field validation and can't interpret what species *care about*
- **Sensor networks** are isolated—they don't connect to broader conservation questions
- EcoScan is the **first unified layer** that makes all three speak the same language

### "How is the AI different?"
- We're not trying to out-vision computer vision (other teams already do that well)
- We're building **contextual intelligence**: given species vulnerability profiles + habitat stress + user evidence, we weight and combine them
- This is more honest and explainable than black-box accuracy claims

### "What's the business model?"
- **Conservation NGOs** (licensing per-region)
- **Government agencies** (habitat monitoring, endangered species reporting)
- **Land trusts** (evidence-based stewardship)
- **Research institutions** (biodiversity assessment, climate resilience studies)
